OVERVIEW

The Product
Websie of Livique, in particular their online shop and furniture modifier.

The Goal
Evaluate and improve the usability of the Livique online store, identifying pain points and proposing concrete design improvements.

My Role
Conducting usability tests, analyzing qualitative and quantitative data, and formularing UX recommendations in a group of three.

UNDERSTANDING THE USER

To compare the in-store with the online shopping experience, we conducted guerilla interviews with customers near the exit of a Livique store. A total of four interview were held, representing various types of customers.

Interview Goals

  • Understand the preferences of the in-store customers
  • Compare the in-store to the online experience
  • Explore initial pain points of real customers
  • Analyze the customer journey

Customer Journey

With the gathered insights, we developed a Customer Journey Map to visualize the steps a user takes when interacting with a product, service, or website. We highlighted how the users feel at each stage of their shopping experience.

Research Question

Next, we formulated a User Research Question to help us define the focus of our study by outlining what we aimed to understand about users' behaviors. This Question guided our testing approach and data analysis.

How do users experience shopping on the Livique online store?

USABILITY STUDY

To asses the user experience of the Livique online store, we conducted a moderated usability test, followed by a standardized User Experience Questionnaire. Our goal was to uncover obstacles in the shopping process and determine how intiutive users found the website.

Recruitment and Setup

Sic participants were recruited to take part in the usability test, which was conducted either online or in a face-to-face interview lasting around 45 minutes.

The selected participants varied in gender, age, socio-economic status, and whether they were users of the website. This diversity provided a range of perspectives, expectations, and experiences. Each participant used their own familiar device and internet connection, ensuring a more familiar scenario.

Usability Test Process

  1. Brief introduction, emphasizing the participants should think aloud during the test
  2. The tasks were provided either digitaly or printed, with participants instructed to complete them sequentially
  3. Main part, where the tasks were completed without help of the conductor
  4. A short "Probing" session (Krug, 2014, p.126) was conducted, asking participants how they generally felt about the website and specific questions about moments deemed important by the moderator
  5. Closing remarks where participants were thanked and asked to fill out the User Experience Questionnaire

Key Takeaways form UEQ

The User Experience Questionnaire results provided quantitative validation for the usability test finding. Since they compare the Livique website agains a large benchmark dataset (21'178 participants across 466 studies), they help contextualize the usability issues.

Low Originality & Stimulation:

  • The website was perceived as unispiring and unengaging, meaning it lacks elements that make the user experience feel unique or enjoyable.
  • This aligns with usability test participants' neutral stance on the website
  • Implication: Consider interactions feel more unique

Below-Average Attractiveness, Transparency, and Efficiency:

  • The website did not stand out aesthetically
  • Users had difficulty understanding how to complete tasks
  • Users needed more effort than expected
  • Implications: Addressing navigation issues and content clarity

Above-Average Control:

  • Despite low engagement, users felt in control
  • This suggest that while finding items was difficult, the interface was not restrictive
  • Implication: Keep intuitive aspects intact, while refining navigation and filtering

Recommendations

  • Priority 1: Introduce a prominent "Configuration Tool" button
  • Priority 2: Standardize classification to ensure products appear under the correct filters
  • Priority 3: Maintain products names consitent through throughout the purchase process
